What is InstantCSS

InstantCSS is easy, rapid, intuitive Utility-first CSS library.
This makes improved your developer experience by implement any designs directly in markup and immediately reflect feedback.
In addition, InstantCSS parses HTML documents at runtime and builds styles, so it can completely replace style files that become bloated whenever updated with a 2.3kb js file(based on gzip).

How to use

Install InstantCSS and add inside your head tag. that's all
<head>
    . . . . . .
    <script src="/instant-css.js"></script>
    <style instant-css="v0.5.0">
        . . . . . .
        .bgc\=red{background-color:red}
        .c\=white{color:white}
    </style>
</head>
<body>
    . . . . . .
    <div class="bgc=red c=white">Hello</div>
</body>

Basic

InstantCSS basically build the class name of the HTML element as a styles.
Class names can't contain spaces" ", so underscores"_" must be used instead of spaces" ".
Using semicolon";" allows multiple styles to be applied to one class.
<head>
    . . . . . .
    <script src="/instant-css.js"></script>
    <style instant-css="v0.5.0">
        . . . . . .
        .background-color\:red{background-color:red}
        .border\:1px_solid{border:1px solid}
        .color\:blue\;font-size\:8px\!important{color:red;font-size:14px!important}
    </style>
</head>
<body>
    . . . . . .
    <span class="background-color:red">raw</span>
    <span class="border:1px_solid">use space</span>
    <span class="color:red;font-size:8px!important">multi style</span>
</body>

Selector & Pseudo Class

Using slash"/" allows selector to be applied to a class.
Regardless of the type of selector, there is no singularity except that the selector written before the slash"/" is appended to the class name.
<head>
    . . . . . .
    <script src="/instant-css.js"></script>
    <style instant-css="v0.5.0">
        . . . . . .
        ._span\.child\/bg\=red span.child{background-color:red}
        .\:active\/bg\=blue\;c\=red:active{background-color:blue;color:red}
    </style>
</head>
<body>
    . . . . . .
    <div class="_span.target/bgc=red">
        <span>child</span>
        <span class="target">target</span>
        <span>child</span>
    </div>
    <span class=":active/bgc=blue;c=red">pseudo class</span>
</body>

Abbreviation

In addition to the basic syntax, InstantCSS supports several abbreviations for faster coding.
There's no problem with coding if you don't know the abbreviation, but using the abbreviation makes things a lot faster.
<head>
    . . . . . .
    <script src="/instant-css.js"></script>
    <style instant-css="v0.5.0">
         . . . . .
        .background-color\=red{background:red}
        .bgc\=blue{background-color:blue}
        .pointer{cursor:pointer}
        .bg\=blue{background:blue}
        .w\=10{width:10em}
        .fs\=calc\(10px\/2\){font-size:calc(10px / 2)}
        .c\=\-\-primary{background:var(--primary)}
    </style>
</head>
<body>
    . . . . . .
    <div class="background=red">"=" for ":"</div>
    <span class="bgc=red">key abbreviation</span>
    <span class="pointer">value abbreviation</span>
    <div class="bg=blue w=10">skip "em"</div>
    <span class="fs=calc(10px/2)">skip calc space</span>
    <div class="bg=--primary">var func</div>
</body>

Priority

Using utility-first css can greatly reduce css conflicts, but still sometimes messes up the order of applying styles.
Instant supports the ability to easily adjust style priorities using the exclamation mark"!".
<head>
    . . . . . .
    <script src="/instant-css.js"></script>
    <style instant-css="v0.5.0">
        . . . . . .
        [class][class][class].c\=yellow\!\!\!{color:yellow}
        [class][class].c\=orange\!\!{color:orange}
        [class].c\=red\!{color:red}
        .\>span\/c\=red>span{color:red}
        .c\=blue{color:blue}
        [class].c\=blue{color:blue}
    </style>
</head>
<body>
    . . . . . .
    <span class="c=yellow!!! c=orange!! c=red!">im yellow</span>
    <div class=">span/c=red">
        <span>im red</span>
        <span class="c=blue">im red</span>
        <span class="c=blue!">im blue</span>
    </div>
</body>

Media Query

To apply a media query, you can write a condition between two at"@" in before the class name.
You can use the logical join function by using the exclamation mark"!" instead of not and the ampersand"&" instead of and.
Also, in media queries, you can shorten typing by using some abbreviations such as "md" or "dark" and range expressions using tilde"~".
<head>
    . . . . . .
    <script src="/instant-css.js"></script>
    <style instant-css="v0.5.0">
        . . . . . .
        .c\=red{color:red}
        .fs\=16px{font-size:16px}
        [class].fs\=16px\!{font-size:16px}
        @media (max-width:767px){
            .\@max-width\=767px\@fs\=12px{font-size:12px}
            .\@max-width\=767px\@\:active\/fs\=12px:active{font-size:1.25em}
        }
        @media not screen and (min-width:640px) and (max-width:767px){
            .\@\!screen\&min-width\=640px\&max-width\=767px\@fs\=10px{font-size:10px}
        }
        @media not screen and (min-width:640px) and (max-width:767px){
            .\@width\=640\~767px\@fs\=10px{font-size:10px}
        }
        @media (min-width:768px){
            .\@md\@fs\=12px{font-size:12px}
        }
    </style>
</head>
<body>
    . . . . . .
    <span class="@max-width=767px@fs=12px">default</span>
    <span class="@max-width=767px@:active/fs=1.25">with selector</span>
    <span class="@!screen&min-width=640px&max-width=767px@fs=10px">not, and</span>
    <span class="@width=640~767px@fs=10px">range</span>
    <span class="@md@fs=12px">abbreviation</span>
    <span class="c=red fs=16px @md@fs=12px">if width >= 768: im 12px</span>
    <span class="c=red fs=16px! @md@fs=12px">im 16px</span>
</body>

Dark Mode

InstantCSS dynamically builds stylesheets based on the "THEME" values in local storage.
You can easily apply a dark mode design to elements by using "@dark@" to apply dark mode as a media query and manipulating local storage.
<head>
    . . . . . .
    <script src="/instant-css.js"></script>
    <style instant-css="v0.5.0">
        . . . . . .
        .c\=red{color:red}
        /* @media (prefers-color-scheme:dark)is ignored */
    </style>
</head>
<body>
    . . . . . .
    <span class="c=red @dark@c=blue">im red</span>
</body>
<script>
    . . . . . .
    localStorage.setItem("THEME", "LIGHT") // if THEME is not DARK and true
    window["instantCss"]() // update style sheet
</script>
<head>
    . . . . . .
    <script src="/instant-css.js"></script>
    <style instant-css="v0.5.0">
        . . . . . .
        .c\=red{color:red}
        /* @media (prefers-color-scheme:dark)is enabled */
            .\@dark\@c\=blue{color:blue}
    </style>
</head>
<body>
    . . . . . .
    <span class="c=red @dark@c=blue">im blue</span>
</body>
<script>
    . . . . . .
    localStorage.setItem("THEME", "DARK") // if THEME is DARK
    window["instantCss"]() // update style sheet
</script>
<head>
    . . . . . .
    <script src="/instant-css.js"></script>
    <style instant-css="v0.5.0">
        . . . . . .
        .c\=red{color:red}
        @media (prefers-color-scheme:dark){
            .\@dark\@c\=blue{color:blue}
        }
    </style>
</head>
<body>
    . . . . . .
    <span class="c=red @dark@c=blue">im follow system</span>
</body>
<script>
    . . . . . .
    localStorage.removeItem("THEME") // if THEME is false
    window["instantCss"]() // update style sheet
</script>
