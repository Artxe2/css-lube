Atomic for CSS Lube

Folder structure

First of all, CSS Lube divides components into three layers.
v src
	v components
		v cells
		v organs
		v parts
. . . . . .
"cells" corresponds to "atoms" in Atomic Design, "organs" corresponds to "molecules" and "organisms", and "parts" manages components that are essentially the same as "organs" but use global status.
Typically, the "cells" subfolders are separated by the type of component, subfolders of "organs" and "parts" are page-by-page.
v src
	v components
		v cells
			v buttons

			v svgs

		v organs
			v @common

			v docs

			v main
				v sub

		v parts
			v @common

				TS store.ts
			v docs

				TS store.ts
			v main

				TS store.ts

Component Rules

By default, CSS Lube can easily customize the style of components using selectors.
<div class=">div>a/br=.75
		_*/f=blue
		_.c\=\#333/c=blue">
	<GithubStars />
</div>
 
 css-lube 
 0
However, CSS Lube recommends separating components from layouts and delegating layouts to parent components.
To do this, the "width" and "height" of the component should be "100%" or "fit-content", the "margin" should be "0", and the "position" should be any property other than "absolute".
Responsive Web Design
